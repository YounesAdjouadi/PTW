Designing, controlling, and optimizing industrial transformations from raw materials to finished products.

Mission and Focus

The Process Engineering Department is central to the chemical, pharmaceutical, petroleum, and environmental industries. We train engineers specialized in **unit operations, reactor design, separation processes, and process safety**.

Our program emphasizes sustainable practices and the economic viability of large-scale industrial systems, preparing graduates to manage complex plants and develop innovative solutions for resource efficiency.

Core Focus Areas

  • **Chemical Engineering:** Thermodynamics, Kinetics, and Transport Phenomena.
  • **Petroleum and Gas Processing:** Refining, separation technologies, and quality control.
  • **Environmental Processes:** Water treatment, waste management, and pollution control.

Academic Programs

We offer specialized tracks catering to the diverse needs of the process industry:

1. Bachelor's (L3) - Common Core: Process Engineering

Foundation in physical chemistry, mass and heat transfer, and industrial calculations.

2. Master's (M) Specializations (2 years)

  • **Master in Petroleum Refining and Petrochemistry:** Focused on crude oil processing and derivative product manufacturing.
  • **Master in Industrial Safety and Environment (HSE):** Risk assessment, pollution prevention, and compliance with industrial standards.
  • **Master in Water Treatment and Industrial Processes:** Advanced methods for purifying water and managing effluent streams.

3. Doctorate (D)

Research focused on areas such as advanced catalytic processes, process intensification, and development of novel membrane separation technologies.

Research and Laboratories

Our research units are key contributors to sustainable industrial development:

  • **Laboratory of Chemical Engineering and Materials (LGC):** Focused on materials science applied to industrial reactors and separation.
  • **Unit for Research in Petroleum and Environmental Processes (URPE):** Dedicated to optimizing fossil fuel processes and developing green technologies.
