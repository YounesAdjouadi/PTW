⚙️ Mechanical Engineering Department

Innovating in design, manufacturing, energy conversion, and fluid mechanics.

Mission and Expertise

The Mechanical Engineering Department focuses on the principles of **motion, energy, and force**. We equip students with the skills to design, analyze, and test machinery, systems, and processes across various industries, from automotive and aerospace to HVAC and power generation.

A strong emphasis is placed on **Computer-Aided Design (CAD), material science, and computational fluid dynamics (CFD)**, ensuring graduates are ready for the modern digital engineering workflow.

Core Focus Areas

  • **Energy and Thermodynamics:** Heat transfer, power plants, and renewable energy devices.
  • **Design and Manufacturing:** Machine design, mechanical parts, and production processes.
  • **Solid Mechanics and Vibrations:** Structural integrity, materials testing, and dynamic system analysis.

Academic Programs

Our academic offerings are structured to provide specialization in high-impact mechanical fields:

1. Bachelor's (L3) - Common Core: Mechanical Engineering

Covers fundamental topics in statics, dynamics, fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, and engineering drawing.

2. Master's (M) Specializations (2 years)

  • **Master in Energy and Fluids:** Focused on thermal power systems, turbomachinery, and renewable energy technologies.
  • **Master in Industrial Design and Manufacturing:** Advanced studies in CAD/CAM, robotics, and quality control in production.
  • **Master in Materials and Structural Mechanics:** Deep dive into advanced composite materials, stress analysis, and structural integrity.

3. Doctorate (D)

Research tracks include advanced composites for extreme environments, optimization of heat exchangers, and development of smart manufacturing systems.

Research and Laboratories

We actively support research through several specialized units:

  • **Laboratory of Mechanics and Structures (LMS):** Focusing on material behavior and structural modeling.
  • **Energy and Thermodynamics Research Unit (UTERM):** Dedicated to improving efficiency in thermal and fluid systems.
